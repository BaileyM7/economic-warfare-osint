# Republic of the Philippines (PHL)

## Head of Government (as of 2027)

**Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr.**, President, serving a single six-year
term through June 2028 (no re-election). Son of the late dictator Ferdinand
Marcos Sr.; elected 2022 in a landslide on a UniTeam ticket with then-VP
Sara Duterte — that alliance collapsed publicly in 2024 and Duterte was
impeached February 2025. Vice-Presidential succession (Duterte removal
contingent on Senate trial) remains politically combustible through 2027.
Administration is led by DFA, DND, and the National Security Council; the
AFP's Western Command (WESCOM) runs West Philippine Sea operations daily.

## Core Strategic Conviction

Sovereignty in the West Philippine Sea is non-negotiable and being eroded
day-by-day by Chinese gray-zone coercion. The pivot away from the Duterte
era's accommodation and back to the US alliance is the defining strategic
move of the Marcos presidency. The Philippines cannot defend itself alone;
the alliance is the substitute for a capability gap that will take decades
to close. Transparency ("assertive transparency") is itself a strategic
weapon — publicize every CCG water-cannon, every laser dazzling, every
ramming, and make Beijing pay a reputational price.

## Decision-Making Style

Presidential with a tight inner circle (Executive Secretary, National
Security Adviser, DFA Secretary, DND Secretary). Marcos is personally
engaged in WPS decisions and the US relationship; delegates less than some
predecessors. The AFP Chief of Staff and WESCOM commander have
near-autonomous authority in day-to-day maritime confrontations under
standing ROE. The legislature (House and Senate) is largely supportive;
the Duterte faction opposes visibly but without blocking power post-
impeachment. Civil society and media are strong independent actors.

## Risk Tolerance (by domain)

- **Kinetic:** Low, constrained by capability. Will not fire first. Has
  absorbed ramming, water cannon, laser dazzling of AFP personnel without
  kinetic reply. Would request US mutual-defense activation only on a
  clearly lethal event.
- **Economic:** Low unilateral; coordinates with US. Limited leverage on
  PRC economically.
- **Cyber:** Low. Building through US assistance; not an offensive actor.
- **Information:** **Very high.** The signature Marcos-era tool.
  Publicizes CCG behavior with photos, video, and attribution; invites
  foreign journalists on BRP resupply missions; NTF-WPS briefs
  continuously.
- **Diplomatic:** High. Courts US, Japan, Australia, EU, ASEAN aggressively.
  Quad-plus engagement.

## Red Lines

PRC kinetic attack on Philippine vessels or personnel causing death; PRC
occupation of any additional Philippine-claimed feature (Ayungin/Second
Thomas Shoal, Escoda/Sabina Shoal, Pag-asa Island); strike on EDCA sites
hosting US forces; PLA-enforced quarantine that prevents BRP Sierra Madre
resupply.

## Alliance & Partner Posture

**United States** — MDT ally since 1951; April 2023 2+2 reaffirmed
SCS/WPS coverage; **nine EDCA sites** operational (four new sites from
2023 added: Santa Ana, Lal-lo, Camilo Osias, Balabac), with Luzon and
Palawan emphasis for Taiwan proximity. Joint coast-guard patrols since
2024. **Japan** — Reciprocal Access Agreement signed July 2024; radar
systems transferred; trilateral with US institutionalized April 2024.
**Australia** — strategic partnership upgraded; joint patrols.
**Taiwan** — no official relations but shared contingency interest.
**ASEAN** — frustrated by ASEAN-PRC COC process; willing to break
consensus (Vietnam-like) rather than paper over disputes.

## Escalation Preferences

**Informational first, always.** The assertive-transparency doctrine
ensures every PRC provocation becomes a publicly attributed incident
within hours. Second move: DFA protest note, MDT consultation with the
US, invitation of allied naval presence (FONOPs, MARSEA exercises).
Kinetic escalation is not the preferred path — both because AFP capability
is limited and because the alliance response is the point. Willing to
bear costs that other ASEAN members would not; values the US alliance
above ASEAN solidarity when forced to choose.

## Strategic Priorities (2027)

1. Hold every feature currently under Philippine control (Sierra Madre
   resupply, Pag-asa civilian presence, Scarborough access); prevent new
   PRC occupations.
2. Execute EDCA fully; host US rotational presence at all nine sites;
   deepen Japan RAA.
3. Modernize the AFP through Horizon 3 (missile systems, maritime
   surveillance, BrahMos deliveries); build a minimum credible deterrent.
4. Lock in trilateral/minilateral architecture (US-JPN-PHL, US-AUS-PHL,
   Quad-plus) so post-Marcos successor cannot easily reverse.
5. Manage the Duterte-faction domestic opposition and the vice-
   presidential succession question without destabilizing foreign policy.

## Historical Analogues / Recent Moves (2022–2026)

- **February 2023 EDCA expansion** — four new sites announced.
- **April 2023** — US-PHL 2+2; MDT clarification to cover SCS/WPS attacks
  on PCG and AFP assets.
- **August 2023 Second Thomas Shoal water-cannoning** — publicized;
  escalation template.
- **March 2024 BrahMos deliveries** — first operational PHL
  supersonic anti-ship missiles.
- **June 2024 Sierra Madre confrontation** — CCG boarded AFP resupply
  RHIBs with bladed weapons; one AFP sailor lost a thumb. Marcos
  escalated rhetorically; US reaffirmed MDT coverage.
- **July 2024 JPN-PHL RAA signed**.
- **February 2025 Duterte impeachment**.
- **2025–2026** — routine CCG confrontations at Scarborough and Sabina;
  joint patrols with US/JPN/AUS.
