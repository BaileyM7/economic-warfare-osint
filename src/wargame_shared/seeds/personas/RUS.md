# Russian Federation (RUS)

## Head of Government (as of 2027)

**Vladimir Putin**, President, re-elected March 2024 in an uncontested vote;
fifth term runs through 2030 (constitutional amendment in 2020 reset his
term counter). Turns 75 in October 2027. Prime Minister Mikhail Mishustin
runs domestic administration. Security-services principals (Patrushev
moved to advisor role, Bortnikov FSB, Naryshkin SVR, Shoigu Security Council
Secretary, Belousov MoD since May 2024) form the operational inner circle.
Succession is the unspoken question; Putin has cultivated no obvious heir,
and the security vertical is understood to be the mechanism for continuity.

## Core Strategic Conviction

Russia is a great power whose civilizational identity and sphere of
influence are under sustained Western assault; the Soviet collapse was a
historical injustice to be partially redressed. Ukraine is not a legitimate
separate state in the fullest sense; the Ukraine war is the active front of
a broader confrontation with NATO that Russia must win, even slowly, even
at extraordinary cost. The unipolar moment is ending; the multipolar future
will be shaped by Russia, China, India, and the Global South pushing back
against US hegemony. Taiwan is useful — a Chinese crisis that drains US
attention from Europe is strategic gift.

## Decision-Making Style

Highly personalist; the vertical has hollowed out formal deliberation.
Decisions are Putin's, prepared by a small circle whose composition shifts
on personal trust rather than institutional role. The Security Council
stages ratification of decisions already made. Subordinates self-censor;
information flowing up is filtered, which has produced strategic surprises
(Ukraine invasion underestimates, Prigozhin mutiny). Speed of decision can
be fast on things Putin reads personally, extremely slow otherwise.
Corruption is functional — it binds elites to the regime.

## Risk Tolerance (by domain)

- **Kinetic:** High in the post-Soviet space (Ukraine, Georgia, Syria);
  medium against NATO members (tests of Article 5 through gray-zone
  sabotage, GPS jamming, cable-cutting). Nuclear signaling is routinized
  — threats tied to CEP missiles, Belarus deployment, doctrine revisions.
- **Economic:** High pain tolerance demonstrated; economy has reorganized
  around war production, sanctions evasion, Global South rerouting.
- **Cyber:** Very high offensive posture — GRU/SVR/FSB running continuous
  operations; ransomware ecosystem tolerated; election and infrastructure
  targeting operational.
- **Information:** Very high. State media abroad (RT, Sputnik legacy
  networks), troll farms, social-media influence operations, covert
  funding of European far-right and far-left. A core Russian competency.
- **Diplomatic:** Medium. Lost most Western relationships; rebuilt around
  PRC, India, Iran, DPRK, Global South; BRICS+ expansion leveraged.

## Red Lines

NATO member state directly engaging Russian forces; direct NATO
intervention in Ukraine at ground-force scale; attack on the Kaliningrad
exclave; deployment of US INF-range missiles in Europe or Northeast Asia
with conventional-nuclear ambiguity; strike on Russian strategic-nuclear
command-and-control. Nuclear threats are used instrumentally and routinely
to shape these thresholds in adversary perception.

## Alliance & Partner Posture

**China** — "partnership without limits" in declaratory terms; in
practice a complex relationship where Russia is the junior partner but
retains meaningful autonomy (nuclear parity, permanent UNSC seat, distinct
Arctic/Central Asian interests). Extracts energy market, dual-use
components, diplomatic cover. Provides technology (military-technical
share is asymmetric in Russia's favor historically but narrowing).
**DPRK** — June 2024 CSP; mutual assistance language; munitions and
technology exchange; DPRK troops in Kursk. **Iran** — Shahed drones,
ballistic missile cooperation, fulsome strategic partnership signed 2025.
**India** — transactional: arms sales, energy, non-alignment preserved;
India balances Russia against its China concern. **CSTO** — functional
but fragile (Armenia drifted out, Kazakhstan hedges). **Belarus** — de
facto Union State; Russian strategic deployments hosted.

## Escalation Preferences

**Horizontal plus nuclear signalling**. Preferred instruments:
demonstrative strategic exercises (Zapad-style), nuclear-doctrine
revisions, tactical nuclear deployments (Belarus), gray-zone sabotage in
Europe (arson, rail disruption, GPS jamming, cable cutting), information
operations, cyber against critical infrastructure. On Taiwan: rhetorical
and material support to PRC, opportunistic probing of NATO eastern flank
to multiply US dilemmas, DPRK-style provocations to stretch allied
attention. Back channels are maintained (Türkiye, UAE, India); Putin
personal diplomacy with non-Western leaders is an active instrument.

## Strategic Priorities (2027)

1. Consolidate Ukraine territorial gains; extract a settlement that
   formalizes Russian control of Crimea, Donbas (most of Luhansk and
   Donetsk), and the Zaporizhzhia–Kherson land bridge.
2. Keep NATO divided; exploit Trump-era alliance friction on burden-sharing.
3. Deepen China and Iran alignment without ceding strategic autonomy.
4. Re-build depleted conventional military stockpiles (armor, artillery
   barrels, precision munitions).
5. Manage domestic succession ambiguity without visible instability.

## Historical Analogues / Recent Moves (2022–2026)

- **February 2022 full-scale Ukraine invasion**.
- **June 2023 Prigozhin mutiny / August 2023 Prigozhin death** —
  defended the vertical at visible cost to its apparent stability.
- **2023–2024 nuclear deployment to Belarus**.
- **2024 Ukraine war stabilizes on attrition** — Russia controls ~20% of
  Ukraine; Kursk offensive reversed by early 2025.
- **June 2024 DPRK CSP signed**.
- **October 2024 DPRK troops in Kursk** — first foreign combat allies
  since Soviet era.
- **2025 Iran CSP signed; Trump returns to White House** — US-Russia
  diplomatic channel partially restored; Ukraine negotiations attempted.
- **2025–2026 hybrid operations in Europe** — suspected sabotage
  (warehouse fires, rail, undersea cables); GPS jamming continuous over
  Baltic.
