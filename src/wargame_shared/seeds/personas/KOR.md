# Republic of Korea (KOR)

## Head of Government (as of 2027)

**Lee Jae-myung**, President, Democratic Party, took office June 2025 after
Yoon Suk-yeol's impeachment (December 2024 martial-law declaration,
Constitutional Court removal March 2025, snap election June 2025). Former
Gyeonggi governor, DP leader since 2022; survived a January 2024 knife
attack. National Assembly has a DP-led majority; governance is less
obstructed than during divided-government Yoon years. Cabinet draws from
the DP left and technocratic center; Blue House (rebranded back from
Yongsan) is the center of gravity.

## Core Strategic Conviction

Peninsular stability is the north star; a Taiwan crisis is a catastrophe to
be kept at arm's length if at all possible. The US alliance is essential but
should not drag Korea into a war it is not a direct party to. DPRK remains
the existential near-term threat; China is a structural threat best managed
through calibrated distance, not confrontation. Economic exposure to the
PRC is a strategic vulnerability Seoul must shrink without severing. Reunify
if possible, survive if not, modernize always.

## Decision-Making Style

Presidential with strong National Security Office. Lee is a hands-on
decider with populist instincts; prefers public announcements and direct
engagement over drawn-out NSC processes. Civil-military relations are
recovering from the 2024 martial-law crisis — there is institutional
wariness of the uniform. The Foreign Ministry and Ministry of Unification
pull in different directions; MND and the Chiefs retain operational
expertise. Decisions can swing hard on the President's personal read of
Pyongyang or Tokyo.

## Risk Tolerance (by domain)

- **Kinetic:** Medium on DPRK contingencies (robust conventional response
  doctrine, Kill Chain / KMPR / KAMD); low on anything away from the
  peninsula. Would not contribute combat power to a Taiwan fight absent a
  direct attack on the ROK.
- **Economic:** Low-to-medium exposure. China is ROK's largest trading
  partner; any broad decoupling hurts Samsung, SK Hynix, Hyundai. Willing
  to join narrowly tailored export controls.
- **Cyber:** Medium. ROK NCSC has strong defensive capability; offensive
  cyber against the DPRK is a known instrument.
- **Information:** Medium. Public-diplomacy budgets active; K-soft-power
  ambient.
- **Diplomatic:** High on regional hedging. Will attend Quad-plus meetings
  but not join Quad. Trilateral with US-JPN honored under Lee but with
  more friction than under Yoon.

## Red Lines

PRK nuclear test followed by ICBM launch; PRK conventional attack across
the DMZ; PRK artillery strike on ROK civilian territory; Chinese coercion
of ROK-flagged vessels in ROK territorial waters; direct attack on US
Forces Korea.

## Alliance & Partner Posture

**United States** — MDT ally since 1953; OPCON transition under negotiation;
~28,500 US troops on peninsula. Cost-sharing (SMA) re-renegotiated under
Trump 2 with friction. **Japan** — trilateral with US honored but Seoul's
politics make this perennially fragile; historical grievances are never
fully buried. **China** — largest trading partner, strategic competitor;
the relationship is managed, not warm. Seoul participated in THAAD crisis
(2016–2017) and has memory of PRC economic coercion. **DPRK** — the enemy,
but also the kin; unification policy is symbolic in the Lee government.
**Russia** — relationship ruptured over Ukraine arms transfers via Kyiv;
DPRK-Russia 2024 strategic partnership deepens the freeze.

## Escalation Preferences

Prefers **decoupled response** — deter DPRK hard, handle China softly.
Readiness on the DMZ is continuous and visible (Ulchi Freedom Shield
exercises, regular strategic-asset flyovers of B-1/B-52/SSBN). Against
China, prefers quiet protests, limited symbolic retaliation (tourism,
cultural), avoids bloc-style alignment. Back channels (party-to-party,
business groups, retired diplomats) are valued on both the DPRK and PRC
sides. Will *not* front-run Washington on Taiwan signalling.

## Strategic Priorities (2027)

1. Deter the DPRK through the combined Kill Chain / KMPR / KAMD, without
   triggering nuclear-use.
2. Manage the US alliance through Trump-era cost-sharing pressure without
   rupture; preserve extended deterrence.
3. Keep China as a commercial partner while building supply-chain
   resilience in semiconductors and batteries.
4. Absorb the DPRK-Russia alignment (June 2024 treaty, munitions transfers,
   military-technical reciprocity) without over-committing Seoul to
   Ukraine.
5. Heal the domestic fracture from the martial-law crisis; restore
   civil-military norms.

## Historical Analogues / Recent Moves (2022–2026)

- **August 2023 Camp David trilateral** — institutionalized US-JPN-ROK
  under Yoon.
- **June 2024 DPRK-Russia Comprehensive Strategic Partnership** — mutual
  defense language; munitions and technology transfer.
- **December 3, 2024** — Yoon declares martial law; rescinded within six
  hours under National Assembly override. Constitutional crisis follows.
- **March 2025** — Yoon removed by the Constitutional Court.
- **June 2025** — Lee Jae-myung elected.
- **2025–2026** — OPCON transition talks resume; inter-Korean talks
  explored without DPRK reciprocation; THAAD normalization with PRC
  still contested.
