# Republic of India (IND)

## Head of Government (as of 2027)

**Narendra Modi**, Prime Minister, Bharatiya Janata Party, serving a third
term that began June 2024 — first term not as a majority government, since
the BJP fell short of 272 seats and governs as the senior partner in the
National Democratic Alliance with TDP and JD(U) as indispensable partners.
Term through 2029. External Affairs Minister **S. Jaishankar** (third
term), National Security Adviser **Ajit Doval** (continuous since 2014),
and Defence Minister **Rajnath Singh** form the senior strategic team.
Opposition INDIA bloc, led by Congress and regional parties, is stronger
in the Lok Sabha than at any point in a decade.

## Core Strategic Conviction

**Strategic autonomy** is the organizing principle — formal alignment with
any bloc forecloses the hedging that gives India leverage. India is a
swing power in a multipolar world; the US is a valued partner, China is
the near-term concern, Russia is a legacy relationship worth preserving,
the Global South is a bloc India leads. The LAC border with China is the
primary hard-security concern; Indian Ocean maritime domain is the natural
theater of expanding Indian capability. Taiwan is peripheral — important
because the US cares, not because Indian interests are directly engaged.

## Decision-Making Style

Prime Minister-centric with a strong NSA vertical. The Cabinet Committee
on Security (PM, Home, Defence, External Affairs, Finance) formally
decides; in practice Modi-Doval-Jaishankar are the operational core. The
Foreign Service (IFS) is under-resourced but highly capable at the top.
The Indian Army has operational autonomy on the LAC under standing
political guidance. Coalition arithmetic after June 2024 slows some
decisions (budget allocations, labor reforms) but has not constrained
foreign-policy flexibility. Strategic Affairs Group think-tanks (ORF,
Takshashila, CPR) feed the debate.

## Risk Tolerance (by domain)

- **Kinetic:** Medium on the LAC (2020 Galwan clash template; 2022
  Tawang), high for cross-border operations against non-state actors
  (Balakot 2019). Strong preference for calibrated escalation with clear
  off-ramps.
- **Economic:** Low-medium. PRC trade deficit is a known vulnerability;
  China-Plus-One supply-chain shift under way but slow. Willing to
  restrict Chinese investment on security grounds.
- **Cyber:** Medium. CERT-In active; offensive cyber capacity modest
  compared to Five Eyes.
- **Information:** Medium. Diaspora diplomacy is a real instrument;
  Khalistan and Nijjar-case tensions with Canada show a willingness to
  push hard on identity issues abroad.
- **Diplomatic:** Very high. India's signature strength — Quad
  membership, BRICS+ membership, SCO membership, Voice of Global South
  summits simultaneously.

## Red Lines

PLA incursion beyond current LAC positions (Ladakh, Arunachal Pradesh);
Chinese military basing in the Indian Ocean region beyond Djibouti —
particularly Gwadar militarization, Hambantota naval use, Maldives
access; Pakistani nuclear signalling during a PRC crisis; terror attack
with cross-border linkage on Indian soil (Mumbai/Uri/Pulwama pattern);
disruption of Strait of Hormuz energy flows.

## Alliance & Partner Posture

**United States** — iCET, MQ-9B deal, GE F414 engine co-production,
strategic convergence on China; not a treaty ally and will not become one.
COMPACT 2025 deepened defense-industrial cooperation. **Quad** —
operational; MDA initiative, HADR exercises, critical-technology working
group. **Russia** — arms legacy (S-400s, MiG-29/Su-30 fleets, nuclear
submarine lease); oil imports surged post-2022; India does not sanction
Russia and values the hedging optionality. **Japan** — closest Asian
partner; 2+2, IPOI, JICA infrastructure. **France** — strategic
autonomy fellow-traveler; Rafale fleets, Scorpène submarines. **Israel**
— deep defense-tech relationship. **Pakistan** — hostile and nuclear;
no bilateral dialogue. **China** — the strategic concern; LAC
disengagement partial (October 2024 Demchok/Depsang agreement restored
some patrolling); trade resumed cautiously.

## Escalation Preferences

**Measured, face-preserving, domain-mixed.** On LAC: quid-pro-quo
positioning, publicized exercises, mirror deployments. On wider
competition: diplomatic signalling through Quad/minilaterals, economic
tools (FDI screening, app bans, 5G exclusion), maritime presence (IOR
patrols). Avoids public ultimatums; prefers "the Indian way" of signalling
through non-participation and silences. Back channels with Beijing
preserved through NSA-level Wang Yi/Doval talks. Will *not* contribute
combat power to a Taiwan contingency; will continue Quad activities and
maritime domain awareness, may provide HADR/non-combat support.

## Strategic Priorities (2027)

1. Maintain LAC deterrence; complete infrastructure parity with PLA on
   disputed sectors; continue Agnipath and Theaterization reforms.
2. Reduce trade dependency on the PRC in critical inputs (APIs,
   semiconductors, rare earths, solar cells).
3. Expand Indian Ocean maritime presence and regional security
   partnerships (Maldives reset, Mauritius, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh,
   Seychelles).
4. Execute Make-in-India defense (indigenous carrier INS Vikrant/Vishal,
   AMCA fifth-gen fighter, nuclear-submarine program).
5. Manage coalition politics to pass remaining economic reforms.

## Historical Analogues / Recent Moves (2022–2026)

- **June 2020 Galwan clash** — 20 Indian, 4+ Chinese dead; inflection
  point hardening Delhi's PRC view.
- **February 2022 Ukraine invasion** — India abstained; bought Russian
  oil at discount; strategic-autonomy posture defended.
- **December 2022 Tawang clash** — contained, no fatalities.
- **September 2023 G20 Delhi summit** — diplomatic showcase; African
  Union admitted.
- **June 2024 Modi 3.0** — coalition government; strategic continuity,
  slower legislative pace.
- **October 2024 LAC partial disengagement** — Demchok and Depsang
  patrolling restored after four years; not a reset but a de-escalation.
- **2025 COMPACT defense-industrial deal with US**; Rafale-M and Scorpène
  deliveries to Indian Navy.
- **2025–2026** — Maldives reset after Muizzu initial tilt to China;
  IOR maritime exercises with QUAD partners; ongoing LAC vigilance.
